# SEO and GEO Strategy Principles

## Current Source Baseline

Primary sources checked while creating this skill:

- Google Search Central, "Creating helpful, reliable, people-first content": prioritize helpful, reliable content for people; self-assess originality, depth, trust, expertise, first-hand experience, and who/how/why.
- Google Search Central, "Guidance on using generative AI content": AI may support research and structure, but scaled pages without added user value can violate spam policies; focus on accuracy, quality, relevance, metadata, structured data, and context about creation where useful.
- Bing Webmaster Blog, "Introducing AI Performance in Bing Webmaster Tools Public Preview" (February 10, 2026): AI visibility includes citations, cited pages, grounding queries, and page-level citation activity; improvements include depth, clear structure, evidence, freshness, and reduced ambiguity across text, images, and video.

Re-check sources when producing a real client strategy because search and AI answer guidance changes.

## 2026 Strategy Shift

Use this table as the strategic filter:

| Old pattern | Better pattern |
| --- | --- |
| "Top 10" clickbait | In-depth "why" guides |
| Keyword stuffing | Topical authority and semantic SEO |
| Self-promotional lists | Third-party citations and earned PR |
| Generic AI generation | Proprietary data and expert view |
| Chasing rankings | Building entity authority |

## Content Quality Signals to Build

- Original information: proprietary data, interviews, benchmarks, observations, examples, teardown findings, or analysis.
- Comprehensive answers: explain why, how, tradeoffs, alternatives, risks, and decision criteria.
- Clear trust markers: author or reviewer context, sources, methodology, update dates when the content was materially changed, and factual consistency.
- First-hand experience: screenshots, test details, product usage, field observations, implementation notes, client examples, or operational lessons.
- Search intent satisfaction: the reader should not need to return to search because the page dodged the real question.
- Extraction-friendly structure: descriptive headings, short answer blocks, tables, FAQ sections, definitions, comparison matrices, and explicit entity names.

## GEO-Specific Planning

- Treat generative answer visibility as source selection and citation worthiness, not just ranking position.
- Identify the questions an AI system must answer to recommend, compare, define, or explain the brand/category.
- Make the best pages easy to cite: include concise claims, evidence, dates, definitions, named entities, original data, and stable URLs.
- Reduce ambiguity across formats: page copy, images, videos, alt text, captions, schema, and off-site profiles should describe the same entities consistently.
- Prioritize pages that already show citation, impression, or ranking signals before creating net-new assets.
- Use Bing AI Performance data where available: cited pages, citation counts, grounding queries, and visibility trends.

## Entity Authority Planning

- Define canonical facts for the organization, product, people, locations, categories, and offers.
- Align those facts across About pages, author pages, product pages, schema, social profiles, directories, PR, partner pages, and knowledge sources.
- Build co-occurrence between the brand and target topics through expert content, third-party mentions, event pages, podcasts, industry reports, integrations, and customer stories.
- Prefer durable mentions from relevant third-party sources over low-quality directory submissions.

## Roadmap Pattern

30 days:

- Baseline analytics, rankings, AI citations, branded search, and competitor/entity gaps.
- Audit priority pages for thin content, outdated claims, missing evidence, schema gaps, and weak internal links.
- Fix obvious crawl/indexing issues and refresh the highest-opportunity pages.

60 days:

- Publish or refresh pillar pages and supporting "why/how" guides.
- Add evidence assets: original data, expert quotes, case studies, comparison tables, and methodology sections.
- Improve entity consistency across on-site structured data and key external profiles.

90 days:

- Launch earned citation campaigns around proprietary insights.
- Consolidate or prune low-value pages that dilute topical focus.
- Review AI citation/query data and Search Console changes; double down on pages gaining impressions, citations, or high-quality conversions.

## Measurement

- Traditional SEO: impressions, clicks, CTR, average position, indexed pages, crawl errors, non-branded queries, assisted conversions, and revenue or qualified leads.
- GEO/answer visibility: AI citations, cited URLs, grounding queries, share of voice in target prompts, sentiment/context of mentions, and competitor inclusion.
- Authority: branded search growth, third-party mentions, backlinks from relevant sources, entity consistency, author/topic recognition, and referral quality.
- Content quality: engagement, scroll depth where available, return-to-search indicators where inferable, conversions, expert review status, and freshness.

## Anti-Patterns

- Publishing many AI-generated pages with no original evidence.
- Rewriting competitor articles without adding new value.
- Creating listicles mainly to capture keywords.
- Stuffing exact-match phrases into headings and body copy.
- Using fake authors, fake review signals, or unsourced claims.
- Treating structured data as a substitute for helpful content.
- Reporting only rankings when the real goal is authority, citations, qualified demand, and conversions.
