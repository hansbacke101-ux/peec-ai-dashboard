# SEO GEO Strategy

## Overview

Use this skill to produce a practical strategy for improving traditional search visibility and generative answer visibility. Treat GEO as an extension of strong SEO and content authority, not as a shortcut or guarantee of citations.

Before making recommendations, check whether current search-engine guidance has changed. Prefer primary sources such as Google Search Central, Bing Webmaster guidance/blogs, schema.org, platform documentation, and the client's own analytics/search data.

## Strategy Frame

Anchor every strategy in this shift:

- Replace "Top 10" clickbait with in-depth "why" guides.
- Replace keyword stuffing with topical authority and semantic SEO.
- Replace self-promotional lists with third-party citations and earned PR.
- Replace generic AI generation with proprietary data and expert viewpoints.
- Replace chasing rankings with building entity authority.

Load [references/strategy-principles.md](references/strategy-principles.md) when creating a full strategy, audit checklist, content roadmap, or measurement plan.

## Workflow

1. Establish the business context: target audience, market, geography, product/category, conversion goals, competitors, and priority topics.
2. Baseline current visibility: organic queries, ranking pages, traffic quality, branded/non-branded demand, AI answer citations, cited pages, share of voice, sentiment, and entity mentions where data is available.
3. Diagnose content quality: identify pages that are thin, generic, over-optimized, outdated, self-promotional, uncited, or missing first-hand evidence.
4. Map topic authority: define pillar topics, supporting clusters, user questions, semantic entities, internal links, author expertise, and missing "why/how/when" depth.
5. Plan citation-worthy assets: prioritize original research, proprietary data, benchmarks, expert interviews, case studies, tools, templates, methodology pages, and comparison pages with evidence.
6. Plan entity authority: align brand, product, people, organization, location, and category facts across the site, structured data, profiles, directories, knowledge sources, and third-party mentions.
7. Plan technical and extraction improvements: improve crawlability, canonicalization, index coverage, schema, headings, summaries, tables, FAQs, media metadata, freshness, and page experience.
8. Plan distribution: identify earned PR targets, industry citations, partner mentions, analyst/listing opportunities, expert contributions, community references, and update announcements.
9. Define governance: require human review for AI-assisted content, visible authorship where appropriate, sourcing, fact checks, update cadence, and no scaled low-value page generation.
10. Define measurement: track Search Console, Bing Webmaster Tools, AI visibility/citation tools, server logs if available, branded search, referral quality, conversions, and manual prompt/query checks.

## Output Shape

When asked for a strategy, return:

- **Diagnosis:** what is limiting visibility now.
- **Strategic thesis:** the main bet for improving search and AI answer presence.
- **Prioritized roadmap:** 30/60/90-day actions with owners or workstreams when possible.
- **Content plan:** pages to create, consolidate, refresh, prune, or support with internal links.
- **Authority plan:** evidence, experts, citations, partnerships, PR, and entity consistency.
- **Technical plan:** schema, crawl/indexing, page structure, and extraction improvements.
- **Measurement plan:** metrics, tools, cadence, and expected leading indicators.
- **Risks and constraints:** assumptions, missing data, areas requiring current verification, and what not to promise.

## Rules

- Do not promise specific rankings, AI citations, or traffic outcomes.
- Do not recommend mass-producing pages from AI prompts.
- Do not recommend hiding text, cloaking, fake reviews, fake authors, synthetic citations, or link schemes.
- Prefer fewer, stronger pages over many shallow pages.
- Prefer concrete evidence and named entities over generic SEO copy.
- If source data is missing, state assumptions and propose the minimum data needed to sharpen the strategy.
